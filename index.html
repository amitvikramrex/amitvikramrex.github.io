Research Experience

  • 2024 Assessing the effects of equi-biaxial and uni-axial tensile pre-straining paths on fatigue crack propagation behaviour of 2024-T4 aluminium alloy.
    • Objective: Investigate
    • Advisor: Dr. Akhilendra Singh, Associate Professor, Department of Mechanical Engineering, IIT Patna and Dr. Surajit Kumar Paul Associate Professor, Department of Mechanical Engineering, IIT Patna.
  • 2024 Assessing notch fatigue performance of aluminium alloy AA2024-T4 after uni-axial and equi-biaxial tensile pre-straining.
    • Objective: Investigate the effect of various pre-straining paths on notch fatigue behavior in AA2024 using a novel physics-based method that predicts notch fatigue life using the cyclic plastic zone. Using high cycle fatigue (HCF) experiments and the Theory of Critical Distance (TCD) with the cyclic plastic zone (CPZ) concept, the proposed method accurately predicts fatigue life.
    • Advisor: Dr. Akhilendra Singh, Associate Professor, Department of Mechanical Engineering, IIT Patna and Dr. Surajit Kumar Paul Associate Professor, Department of Mechanical Engineering, IIT Patna.
  • 2024 Analyzing the effect of tensile pre-Straining on fatigue behavior of AA2024-T4 aluminium alloy.
    • Objective: Investigate how uniaxial and equi-biaxial tensile pre-straining affect the tensile properties and fatigue behavior of AA2024-T4 aluminum alloy. EBSD analysis revealed increased lattice distortion in pre-strained specimens, while both pre-straining methods enhanced tensile strength, yield stress, and fatigue strength. The Theory of Critical Distance (TCD) accurately predicted fatigue life for notched specimens within a Â±2 scatter band.
    • Advisor: Dr. Akhilendra Singh, Associate Professor, Department of Mechanical Engineering, IIT Patna and Dr. Surajit Kumar Paul Associate Professor, Department of Mechanical Engineering, IIT Patna.
  • 2024 Assessing the effect of tensile pre-strain on the low cycle fatigue performance of AA2024-T4 aluminum alloy.
    • Objective: Examine the low cycle fatigue performance of AA2024-T4 aluminum alloy under different tensile pre-straining conditions. The method involved comparing fatigue life and cyclic stress-strain responses, with uniaxial pre-straining improving fatigue life, while equi-biaxial pre-straining showed similar fatigue life to non-pre-strained specimens, and significant lattice distortion was observed in equi-biaxial pre-strained specimens.
    • Advisor: Dr. Akhilendra Singh, Associate Professor, Department of Mechanical Engineering, IIT Patna and Dr. Surajit Kumar Paul Associate Professor, Department of Mechanical Engineering, IIT Patna.
  • 2024 Assessing the influence of pre-Strain on tensile response and crash performance of extra deep drawing steel at different strain rates.
    • Objective: Ivestigates how pre-strain affects the tensile behavior of extra deep drawing (EDD) steel at different strain rates. Using uniaxial pre-strain and tensile tests, the research examines changes in yield stress, ultimate tensile strength, elongation, and strain energy absorption, finding that pre-strain improves strength and energy absorption but reduces elongation. Additionally, a validated model was used to predict crash performance of pre-strained EDD steel in automotive components.
    • Advisor: Dr. Surajit Kumar Paul Associate Professor, Department of Mechanical Engineering, IIT Patna.
